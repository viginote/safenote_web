Privacy Policy

How SafeNote is designed to collect, process and protect limited incident information.

Important: These documents are operational legal templates for the SafeNote platform and should be reviewed by a South African legal professional before full public rollout.

1. Purpose of this Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how SafeNote is designed to collect, process, store and use information submitted through the SafeNote website, public reporting map, NHW/CPF portal and admin systems.

2. What SafeNote Is

SafeNote is a community awareness and incident logging platform. It is not a law enforcement agency, emergency service, private security company, armed response provider, dispatch centre, investigation unit or official SAPS reporting channel.

3. Information Collected Through Public Reports

Public incident reports may capture:

  • Incident type
  • Severity level
  • GPS coordinates or mapped location
  • Timestamp
  • Optional short note entered by the reporter
  • Technical anti-abuse data, such as hashed IP data for rate-limiting and abuse prevention

4. Information SafeNote Does Not Intend to Collect for Public Reports

  • Names
  • ID numbers
  • Phone numbers
  • Email addresses
  • Public user profiles
  • Reporter identity displayed to the public

5. Optional Notes and Personal Information

Users must not submit names, phone numbers, home addresses, ID numbers, vehicle registration details linked to private persons, defamatory accusations, threats or personal details of victims, suspects, witnesses, children or vulnerable persons unless legally necessary and specifically requested by authorised structures.

6. Location Data

Location data is used to map incidents, identify patterns and support community awareness. Public views may be delayed, rounded, aggregated or limited for safety, privacy and abuse-prevention purposes. Verified NHW/CPF and admin views may access more detailed internal data for legitimate community safety planning.

7. NHW / CPF Access

Verified community structures may receive access codes to view additional dashboards, heatmaps, statistics, exports and operational summaries. Access may be revoked at any time if misused.

8. Use of Information

SafeNote information may be used to support community awareness, hotspot analysis, trend reporting, community safety planning, NHW/CPF briefings, internal moderation, abuse prevention and anonymised or aggregated exports.

9. Storage and Security

SafeNote uses reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect platform information. No internet-connected system can be guaranteed fully secure. Users must avoid submitting unnecessary personal or sensitive information.

10. POPIA Alignment

SafeNote is designed around principles of minimal collection, purpose limitation, restricted access, privacy-aware reporting and responsible processing. POPIA promotes the protection of personal information processed by public and private bodies and establishes minimum requirements for lawful processing.

11. Data Retention

SafeNote may retain reports for operational, statistical, moderation, audit, abuse-prevention and community reporting purposes. Test data may be cleared by administrators. Formal retention periods should be confirmed before full public rollout.

12. Data Subject Requests

Where applicable and technically possible, individuals may request access, correction or deletion of personal information associated with them. Because public reports are intended to be anonymous and minimal, SafeNote may not always be able to identify a specific reporter or report without supporting details.

13. Contact

Official contact details will be added once the SafeNote domain email and Google Workspace account are active.
