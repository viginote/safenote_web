Acceptable Use Policy

Rules for good-faith reporting and prevention of misuse, harassment, sabotage or exploitation.

Important: These documents are operational legal templates for the SafeNote platform and should be reviewed by a South African legal professional before full public rollout.

1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy protects SafeNote, communities, reporters, NHW/CPF structures and the public from misuse of community safety information.

2. Good-Faith Reporting

Reports must be made in good faith and should be based on genuine observations, credible community awareness or legitimate safety concerns.

3. Do Not Submit Personal Identifiers

  • No names of alleged suspects
  • No private phone numbers
  • No ID numbers
  • No private addresses linked to named individuals
  • No images or descriptions designed to expose private persons unnecessarily
  • No identifying information relating to children or vulnerable persons

4. No Defamation or Accusations

Users must not use SafeNote to accuse named persons, families, households, businesses or groups of criminal conduct. Reports should describe observed incidents or concerns without unnecessary personal identification.

5. No Threats, Harassment or Vigilantism

SafeNote may not be used to threaten, harass, intimidate, track, confront, retaliate against or expose any person or group.

6. No Criminal Exploitation

Users may not use SafeNote to identify patrol gaps, exploit community safety information, evade law enforcement, plan offences, mislead communities or sabotage reporting systems.

7. NHW / CPF Responsibilities

Verified users must protect their access codes, avoid sharing restricted data publicly, use information only for legitimate community safety purposes and follow applicable law, CPF procedures, NHW protocols and privacy obligations.

8. Moderation and Removal

SafeNote may moderate, delay, hide, aggregate, redact, remove, reject or archive reports for safety, privacy, abuse-prevention or legal reasons.

9. Access Revocation

SafeNote may revoke public, NHW, CPF or admin access without notice if misuse, suspicious behaviour, unauthorised sharing, abuse or legal risk is suspected.

10. Reporting Misuse
