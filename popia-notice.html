POPIA Notice

SafeNote’s working POPIA-aligned notice for limited incident and location data processing.

Important: These documents are operational legal templates for the SafeNote platform and should be reviewed by a South African legal professional before full public rollout.

1. POPIA Context

The Protection of Personal Information Act, 2013 (POPIA) promotes the protection of personal information processed by public and private bodies and introduces conditions for lawful processing.

2. SafeNote Processing Purpose

SafeNote processes limited incident and location information for community awareness, hotspot mapping, trend analysis, abuse prevention, NHW/CPF insight and platform administration.

3. Data Elements

Public reports may include:

  • Incident type
  • Severity
  • Timestamp
  • Location coordinates
  • Optional short note
  • Hashed technical anti-abuse information

4. Data Minimisation

SafeNote is designed not to require names, phone numbers, ID numbers, emails or public profiles for public incident reports.

5. Special Personal Information

Users must avoid submitting unnecessary special personal information, information about children, health information, sexual assault details, victim identities, suspect identities or other sensitive information. Where sensitive categories are unavoidable, SafeNote may restrict, redact, aggregate or hide the information.

6. Access Control

Public views are restricted and may be delayed or aggregated. NHW/CPF access is controlled by access codes. Admin functions are restricted by an administrator secret.

7. Security Safeguards

SafeNote aims to apply reasonable technical and organisational controls, including restricted access, server-side admin routes, token controls, rate-limiting, hashed IP data for abuse prevention and separation between public and restricted data layers.

8. Operator Responsibilities

SafeNote administrators should maintain secure secrets, revoke unused access codes, review reports for privacy risk, avoid unnecessary exports and ensure that community structures understand acceptable use obligations.

9. Data Subject Rights

Where POPIA applies and identification is possible, data subjects may have rights to request access, correction or deletion of personal information. Because SafeNote is designed around anonymous reporting, identifying a specific report may require sufficient supporting information.

10. Legal Review

Before full public rollout, SafeNote should obtain legal review of its POPIA compliance, terms, privacy policy, retention schedule, security measures and operational procedures.
